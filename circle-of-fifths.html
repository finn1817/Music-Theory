üéº What is the Circle of Fifths?

The Circle of Fifths is a visual representation of key signatures and their relationships. Moving clockwise, each key is a perfect fifth higher than the previous one.

Perfect Fifth Interval: 7 semitones (C to G, G to D, etc.)

üîç How to Use

  • Hover over any key to see detailed information
  • Clockwise: Each step adds one sharp
  • Counterclockwise: Each step adds one flat
  • Inner circle: Relative minor keys
  • Outer circle: Major keys

üéπ Key Relationships

Adjacent Keys: Share most notes in common

Opposite Keys: Share the fewest notes

Relative Major/Minor: Share the same key signature

üéº Modulation

The Circle shows the easiest keys to modulate to:

  • Closely related keys: Adjacent on the circle
  • Relative major/minor: Same key signature
  • Dominant key: One step clockwise
  • Subdominant key: One step counterclockwise
